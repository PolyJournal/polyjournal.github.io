Voter Turnout x Party Victory

The presidential race definitely shows that higher turnout generally leads to a Democratic victory. A big spike is the election of 1992 with the victory of Bill Clinton and Al Gore. But even though Clinton and Gore were reelected the next election, they were reelected with a much lower voter turnout showing just how volatile turnout can be. The graph also exposes how voter turnout has fallen since the 1960s and early 1970s.

In a house election, candidates are much more localised with changes what parties people vote for.
